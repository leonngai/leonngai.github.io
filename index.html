Latest Projects

MedChek

An $8000 award winning idea

Pictured above: Members of the MedChek team (I am second from the left) accepting the Innovation Award at the 2018 Innovation for Health Competition.


Research has shown that adverse drug reactions from prescription medication cost the Canadian healthcare system approximately $35.7 million dollars per year. A major cause of this problem stems from the fact there is currently no proper system in place for patients to receive follow-ups from pharmacists to the drugs that patients take. This 6 week project that entered the 2018 Innovation for Health competition and won the "Best in Category: Innovation" prize tackled that problem by providing a platform for pharmacists to automate the follow-up process via texts to ensure better patient care.

Though the source code is proprietary, more information about the product can be found here.
